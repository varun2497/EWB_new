Importance of Solar Electrification:

More than 1.4 billion people worldwide have no access to electricity. In India, of the 80 million homes in the country, 32.8% households are off-grid. Another 30-40 million homes are near off-grid. It is common for black-outs to last for 10-12 hours a day. Despite great advances in solar photovoltaic technology over the past half-century, it has not been widely adopted for electricity generation. In 2014, the Government of India announced a target generation capacity of 100 GW through solar energy by 2022. In the last three years, the solar power generation capacity has grown by 1 GW per year. In 2015 alone, India is expected to add as much as 5 GW (1.1 GW already commissioned). In 2016, India hopes to add 7-10 GW electricity generation through solar (the government plans to auction 10 GW this year).

Recently, India has witnessed a huge investment in the solar electricity generation sector by global companies from Russia, Japan and USA. Therefore, considering the concerns about rising electricity cost, increased concern for climate change and need to find alternative energy solutions, coupled with considerable government support, solar PV technology is increasingly seen as a viable option for our current and future energy supply.

Need for the Project

The school is an all-girls school run by the Govt. of Tamil Nadu and is situated in Ashok Nagar, Chennai. The strength of the school is approximately 3400 students, and most of the students belong to low income category.

Electricity supply is inadequate in the campus, and the school experiences significant power cuts during the summer season. Also, the students are at times forced to sit outside the rooms for taking classes during power cuts due to poor lighting inside the classrooms during power cuts.

So an off-the-grid electricity generation system using solar energy is required so that lights and fans can be installed in the school to provide the students a comfortable atmosphere for learning. The proposed system will utilize lights and fans running on DC power instead of the usual AC power, to minimize power conversion losses.

Project Goals

Under the Phase-1 of this project, we plan to implement a rooftop solar photovoltaic power plant (polycrystalline type) on the roof of the new block of this school, which has a total of 12 classrooms. The appliances which will be powered by this power plant are tube lights and ceiling fans.

Project Impact

Undertaking this project will improve the study environment of over 3400 girl students every year. This will have a positive impact on the education of girl students coming from a poor background.

Also, installation of solar technology will generate awareness among students, school staff and the society in general. This in turn, will inspire other schools and households to emulate this example and move towards self-sufficiency and sustainability.

By installing the solar power plant, we look forward to a renewable and sustainable source of clean energy which, once installed, involves minimal expenses. Operating and maintenance costs for photovoltaic panels are extremely low, almost negligible, compared to the recurring operating cost of other energy systems.
