Situation in India

India accounts for 58% of world population without access to sanitation facilities. 2.4 million Children die under the age of 5 due to improper sanitation facilities. Open defecation has adverse effects on economic and social development. It is also a major contributor to multiplicity of water and sanitation related diseases as well as groundwater pollution. The productive activities of impoverished population, like schooling, are severely restricted by ill health from contaminated water.

The project is being undertaken so that we could address the problems, mentioned above, that are being caused due to open defecation.

Anticipated Benefits

  • Avoids many water-borne and mosquito-related diseases leading to higher productivity and better economic and social conditions.
  • Ensures growth of children and well-being of Adults.
  • Stops open defecation; clean environment and no contamination of groundwater.
  • Covered sanitation infrastructure; safeguards dignity of humans in the community.
  • Has a minimum life of 50 years and hence, is a long-term solution.

Brief Methodology

Our aim is to target the village outskirts of Chennai, with around 900 families. Major stakeholders will be daily wage workers and florists with an average income of Rs. 5000 per month. These families do not have an access to toilet facilities and resort to open defecation.

The community population will be a direct beneficiary of this project. Each household that currently does not have a toilet will be provided with a toilet to overcome the issue of open defecation. This will play a major role in decreasing the outbreak of sanitation and water related diseases. Each family of the community will be provided with a toilet with water and electric connection. Together with toilet attached bathroom will also be provided for better acceptance from people. It is proposed that an initial amount of Rs. 3000 be collected from each family for setting up of toilet. This is to instill a sense of ownership among the family members to ensure an efficient and optimal use of the resource.

Next Phase

Implementation of 50 toilets in Vilangadupakkam village, Thiruvallur district.

Status of Toilets

Mr. Gokul (Member, EWB Chennai Professional Chapter) along with his team made a visit to village in the month of June to gauge the condition of toilets and they made the following observations :-

  • It has been six months since the toilets were installed and they are functioning quite well.
  • Families using the toilets have completely stopped going for open defecation.
  • Awareness among the locals have increased regarding the benefits of having toilets and therefore, many people have come forward to install toilets in their homes.
